Women of Color:

How Vogue has represented women of every shade over the years

Description:

The comparision investigates inclusion of Women of Color in Vogue magazine.

The magazine has tried to be more race inclusive over the years and has tried to feature more women of darker shades than it used to previously.

However, when we look at the number of darker shades shown compared to that of lighter shades we come across a significant difference between the two.

Left Chart:

Title: Inclusion of darker colors over the years.

Stance: This chart stands for the magazine showing an increase in inclusion of the number of darker shades towards later years.
The spread of colors can been seen over time with the period between 2014-2018 showing a lot of variance as compared to the period between 2000-2004.

Rhetorical Technique used: "Anchoring" user's attention to look for trends showing inclusion of more darker shades in recent years compared to that in previous years.
Right Chart:

Title: Darker shades are only outliers.

Stance: This chart stands against the magazine showing lesser number of darker shades as compared to lighter shades even with apparent inclusion over the years.
When arranged according to their skin's luminance, the darker shades apper as outliers in the cluster of shades featured in the magazine's cover.

Rhetorical Technique used: "Classification" by color to create clusters showing darker colors as outliers compared to the number of lighter colors shown on the covers.
